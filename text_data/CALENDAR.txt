
        Looking In  Puck Building, Houston and Lafayette Streets; (212) 777-5218.
        The 10th Outsider Art Fair opens tonight with a preview to benefit the American Folk Art Museum. Tickets for admission at 5:30 p.m., $200, or, for those 40 and younger, $100; admission at 6:30, $75.
        The show and sale, by 33 dealers, will continue tomorrow from noon to 8 p.m., and on Saturday and Sunday from 11 a.m. to 7 p.m. Admission is $15.
        On Brooklyn's Waterfront  Brooklyn Historical Society; (718) 222-4111, ext. 26.
        Francis Morrone, an architectural historian, will lead a tour by bus and on foot along the waterfront in the Greenpoint, Williamsburg and Red Hook-Gowanus sections of Brooklyn on Saturday starting at 10:30 a.m. Reservations are required. Fee, $25; students and those 55 and older, $22; members, $20.
        Antiques Along the Hudson  Piers 88 and 90, 12th Avenue between 48th and 55th Streets; (212) 255-0020.
        Antique and modern furniture, patriotic memorabilia, porcelain, flags, jewelry, textiles and prints will be offered by 350 dealers at a show and sale on Saturday and Sunday. Open each day 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Admission is $12.
        Museums Reacting  CUNY Graduate Center, 365 Fifth Avenue (34th Street); (212) 683-0023, ext. 21.
        Tonight at 6, the New York chapter of the American Institute of Architects will present a discussion on how cultural institutions have changed the way they present and plan exhibitions, protect artworks and visitors, and develop architectural projects since Sept. 11. Institutions represented will include the Bronx Museum of the Arts, the Museum of Modern Art, the Queens Botanical Garden, the Asia Society, the Whitney Museum of American Art, the South Street Seaport Museum and the New-York Historical Society. Admission is $10; members, $5.
        An Architect on Drawing  Metropolitan Club, 1 East 60th Street; (917) 237-1208.
        On Feb. 7 at 7 p.m., Michael Graves, the architect and designer, will offer a lecture about his work and the importance of drawing in the creative process. Reservations must be made by Feb. 1. Tickets for a reception at 6 and the lecture, $50.
        A dinner to benefit the Institute of Classical Architecture will follow the lecture. Tickets start at $250.
        Community and Quilts  DeCordova Museum and Sculpture Park, 51 Sandy Pond Road, off Route 126, Lincoln, Mass.; (781) 259-8355.
        ''Clara Wainwright: Quiltmaker and Celebration Artist,'' an exhibition of more than 40 quilts and fabric collages, books, installations of painted furniture and photographs, will be at the museum through May 27.
        Ms. Wainwright, whose textiles address themes like politics, humor, environmentalism, music and popular culture, is also known for her efforts to involve community groups in art projects and her planning for events like First Night and the Great Boston Kite Festival.
        Open Tuesday through Sunday, 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Admission is $6; students, those 60 and older, and those age 6 to 12, $4.
