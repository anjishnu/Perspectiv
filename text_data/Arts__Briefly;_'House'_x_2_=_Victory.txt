
        In a night of mostly reruns, two ''House'' repeats made Fox the No. 1 network in the ratings on Tuesday. According to Nielsen's estimates, the 9 p.m. ''House'' episode fared the best of the night among adults 18 to 49, earning 10 million viewers. An earlier ''House'' repeat at 8 drew 6.9 million viewers. CBS ranked second for the night in total viewers with an all-rerun lineup of ''NCIS'' at 8 (11.2 million), ''The Unit'' at 9 (8.8 million), and ''CSI: New York'' at 10 (8.1 million). Meanwhile, NBC posted a second-place performance among adults 18 to 49, winning the 8 p.m. hour in that demographic with the second installment of ''Identity'' (8.2 million). ABC was fourth over all. Its Charlie Brown special at 8, ''I Want a Dog for Christmas'' finished second in total viewers at 8 (8.9 million), but two episodes of ''Big Day'' at 9 (5.2 million) and 9:30 (4.3 million) garnered small audiences.
