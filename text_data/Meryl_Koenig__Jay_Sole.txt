
        Meryl Stacey Koenig, a French-horn player, was married last evening to Jay Daniel Sole, an associate securities analyst. Rabbi Arnold B. Marans performed the ceremony at the Sephardic Temple in Cedarhurst, N.Y., with Cantor David Abikzer participating.
        Mrs. Sole, 28, has played with the National Opera Center, which is a summer festival at Brooklyn College; with the 92nd Street Y Symphonic Workshop Orchestra in Manhattan and the Regina Opera Company in Brooklyn. She is also an adjunct professor of music at Adelphi University and Nassau Community College. She graduated from the University of Pennsylvania and received a master's degree in brass performance from Boston University.
        She is a daughter of Elyce R. Koenig and Judge Leslie R. Koenig of Boynton Beach, Fla. Her father, who is retired, sat on State Family Court in Westbury, N.Y. Her mother retired as a medical research scientist, studying Tay-Sachs disease, at Curative Technology, a biotechnology firm in Hauppauge, N.Y.
        Mr. Sole, 31, is an associate stock research analyst for Morgan Stanley in Purchase, N.Y. He graduated from Oakland University in Rochester, Mich., and received an M.B.A. from Fordham.
        He is a son of Carol Sole and Dr. Gary Sole of Bloomfield Hills, Mich. His father is an ophthalmologist in private practice in Lapeer, Mich.
