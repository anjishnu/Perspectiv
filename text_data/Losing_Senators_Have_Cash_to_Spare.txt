
        One rule of thumb in politics, particularly when locked in a race for your life, is this: Thy campaign account should be depleted -- or well on its way -- by Election Day. But reports filed last week showed that at least two Republican senators defeated in the midterm elections had remaining balances that surpassed six figures each. So what can this money be used for?
        ''They can't use the funds for personal use, like buying personal property or yachts,'' said Michael E. Toner, the chairman of the Federal Election Commission. ''Otherwise, they have wide discretion.'' Money can be given to charities, contributed to candidates or transferred to political parties.
