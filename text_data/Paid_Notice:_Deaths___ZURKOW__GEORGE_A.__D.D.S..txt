
        ZURKOW--George A., D.D.S. Dr. Zurkow, at the age of 91, died peacefully at his home in Wilmington, Delaware on Wednesday, December 13, 2006. Dr. Zurkow devoted most of his professional and personal life to the improvement of the oral health of all the citizens of Delaware. He was the founder of Delaware's innovative DIDER program and was active for over 60 years as a member of the staff of Christiana Care Health Systems. Dr. Zurkow was a past president of the American Association of Endodontics and the Delaware Dental Society, and he was the first dentist to be President of the Delaware Academy of Medicine. Dr. Zurkow also spearheaded the fluoridation of the Wilmington public water supply and was instrumental in establishing the Delaware Technical and Community College's program for training dental assistants. An honored alumni of the University of Pennsylvania School of Dental Medicine, where he was a Clinical Professor for over 25 years. Dr. Zurkow was also a Fellow of the American College of Dentists and of the International College of Dentists and a member of the Pierre Fouchard Society. Dr. Zurkow is survived by his wife, Libby; sisters, Louise Koffsky and Gloria Rubin, son Peter and his wife Erica and daughter, Deborah Zurkow-Debenedetti and her husband Laurent and five grandchildren.
